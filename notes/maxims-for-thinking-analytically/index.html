Maxims for Thinking Analytically

When you are having trouble getting your thinking straight, go to the extreme case

  • Helpful in the following situations
    • To undestand a concept, problem, or idea
      • Identify outliers
    • To assess the best-case or worst-case consequences of a decision
      • Stress test provides a sense of security - if you could pass it

When you are having trouble getting your thinking straight, go to a simple case

  • “Everyhing should be made as simple as possible, but no simpler” Einstein

Don’t take refuge in complexity

  • Are you hiding in complexity to prevent yourself from answering critical questions?

When trying to understand a complex real-world situation, think of an everyday analogue

  • Memorable everyday analogue to effectively communicate ideas

The world is much more uncertain than you think

  • Hindsight bias: a tendency to perceive past events as having to been more predictable than they actually are
  • Recognize and plan accordingly
Probability \ State Known Unknown
Known Risk N / A
Unknown Uncertainty Ignorance
  • “Uncertainty, not risk, is the difficulty regularly before us” - Richard Zeckhauser
  • “Ignorance is an important phenomenon, I would argue, ranking alongside uncertainty, and way above risk. Ignorance achieve its importance, not only by being widespread, but also involving outcomes of great consequence.” - RZ

Think probabilistically about the world

  • Understanding what subjective probability actually mean
    • Based on judgement, not measured
  • Assign probabilities to many things, such as events, in our lives to help us make decisions
    • Quantitative over verbal (likely, unlikely)
  • Update probabilities as new information become available

Uncertainty is the friend of the status quo

  • We tend to stick with our initial/previous choice
  • Status quo bias

Good decision sometime have bad outcomes

  • Decision and outcome should be evaluated separately
Quality of Decision \ Q of Outcome Good Bad
Good You decided well/did well You were unlucky
Bad You were lucky Decided poorly/did poorly

Some decisions have a higher probability of bad outcome

Error of commission should be weighed the same as error of omission

  • Omission bias: EoC is often weighted heavily than EoO

Don’t be limited by the options in front of you

  • Don’t fall for “let me pick your options, and I’ll make the decision for you”

Information is only valuable if it can change your decision

  • Don’t wait for information if it can’t change your decision
